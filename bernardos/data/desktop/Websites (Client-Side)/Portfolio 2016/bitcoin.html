I'm a strong Bitcoin supporter!


First two encounters

I encountered the word "Bitcoin" in early 2013 (secondary two) on an online webmasters forums. I joined that webmasters forums because I wanted to purchase a top-level domain name using a centralized virtual currency. While I was freelancing for the centralized virtual currency, I learnt about Bitcoin. I ignored it.

Sometime later (probably in mid 2013), I saw a thread about Bitcoin on the forums again. The writer stated that he will purchase a .com domain at Namecheap for just 0.01 Bitcoin (about 1 USD at that time). I became interested about Bitcoin and did some research about Bitcoin. I found out that it is a decentralized currency that anyone could use - no age limit like Paypal or credit card - so I started using it. I created my wallet on my computer the next day after reading some online documents about Bitcoin. I found a few websites that give out free Bitcoin (known as faucets) and participated in it. Addtionally, I also joined Bitcointalk (largest Bitcoin community forums) to discuss and learn more about Bitcoin. I only managed to earn 10 cents USD a week from faucets. After knowing that I could not reach 0.01 Bitcoin (1 USD) anytime soon, I stopped visiting those bitcoin-giving faucets and left Bitcoin.

I went back to the "Bitcoin world" again after the price skyrocketed from 100 USD to 1000 USD per coin in early 2014. That also mean, my hard earned 10 cents became 1 USD! I could have make a fortune if I continue to use faucets or buy a few Bitcoin back then. Then I made a batch of Satoshi (smallest unit of Bitcoin) this time. This encounter doesn't last me long though. I left the community again after a few weeks.

Last encounter - become a loyal Bitcoin supporter

After I released my game "Milkacrea", I found out that I need some sort of advertising to bring in new players. Then one of my members suggested me to use Bitcoin when I said that I do not have access to Paypal or Credit Card. I decided to come back to the world of cryptocurrency in late 2014 and searched for ways to earn free Bitcoin fast. To my surprise, I found out that there is something called "signature campaign" at Bitcointalk. It is bascially a campaign for members of Bitcointalk to make posts while wearing advertiser signature tags. I participated in one signature campaign and made about 20 posts in order to receive 4 USD worth of Bitcoin per week . Good money, I told myself. I continue to earn Bitcoin using this method even after my RPG was shutdown till this second. As I become more active, my membership rank increases. As my rank increases, I earn more Bitcoin per post - currently, 0.0008 Bitcoin.

Knowing that the Bitcoin I earned at Bitcointalk cannot do much, I opened my own unique faucet in mid 2015 and named it "BitRevenues". It is a free Bitcoin lottery site, where people can visit the site and claim free tickets without the need to register. Every month we will do a drawing. 60% of the total revenues of that month will be given to three lucky Bitcoin winning addresses. 30% of the revenues will be moved to next round and I will take the leftover 10% for running the free lottery game. It is getting more and more popular everyday. Despite still not earning any profit yet, I am very happy with my achivements - top 1 000 000 Alexa rank worldwide and first page on Bing, DuckDuckGo and Google for the keyword "free Bitcoin lottery".

Today, I am a Hero member of Bitcointalk and I participate in forums actively. Addtionally, I am the proud owner of a Bitcoin related website.
